## Interview explanation

I wanted to understand Akerlof's argument computationally rather than only summarize it. I created a stylized market with good products and lemons. Since buyers could not observe quality, they offered an expected average price. That price was below what good sellers were willing to accept, so good sellers withdrew. The simulation made the feedback loop visible: hidden information lowers expected quality, which lowers prices, which further discourages high-quality sellers.

## How to describe its limitation honestly

This is a deliberately simplified theoretical model using artificial values. It is a proof-of-concept for understanding adverse selection, not an empirical estimate of a real market. The next stage would test different quality shares and add certification, inspection, warranties, or real agricultural-market data.

## Important wording rule

Use **“built,” “implemented,” and “simulated.”** Avoid claiming that you discovered a new result, conducted field research, or validated Akerlof's theory empirically.
